Overview

What is “52 Weeks of Fraud”?

52 Weeks of Fraud is a global training series built around one simple idea: investigators learn best by working real‑world cases — not by clicking through slides.

Every week, you’ll receive access to a new CaseBunker scenario designed from field‑tested patterns of fraud, process failure, and operational loss.

What you get

  • 1 new fraud or loss scenario every week
  • Access to all previously released scenarios
  • Individual and (optional) company leaderboards
  • Weekly recognition and spotlight features
  • Participation badges and completion records

What it costs

  • Free during the Beta season
  • No payment details required to join
  • Upgrade paths for organizations after Beta

Our goal in this first season is simple: build the best investigator training canon on the market and prove its value in the real world.

How it works

One scenario per week. Simple structure. Serious depth.

The weekly cadence

  1. Monday: A new scenario is released in CaseBunker.
  2. Monday–Sunday: Participants complete the scenario at their own pace.
  3. End of week: Scores are locked and weekly standings are updated.
  4. Following week: Highlights and learning points are shared.

Late to the party? No problem. When you join, you get access to all scenarios released so far, and you can work through them on your own schedule.

The scenario format

  • Short narrative setup based on real patterns
  • Evidence, events, and decisions to work through
  • Clear learning objectives for each case
  • Scoring logic tied to judgment, not random guessing
  • Reflection and key takeaways at the end

Scoring & leaderboards

We’re not just measuring speed. We’re measuring judgment.

Every scenario is scored using a structured model inside CaseBunker. The goal isn’t to reward whoever clicks the fastest — it’s to highlight investigators who make strong, defensible decisions.

Example weekly recognition

  • Fastest Correct Completion
  • Highest Accuracy Score
  • Best Decision Path (minimal missteps)
  • Zero‑Hint Completion
  • Most Improved Investigator

Team & company metrics

  • Top Company / Top Team of the Week
  • Average score by organization
  • Participation and completion rates
  • Consistency across multiple weeks

Company‑level metrics are only shared publicly if organizations opt in. Otherwise, they are used to provide private insights to participating teams.

Basic rules

Simple rules. Serious expectations.

52 Weeks of Fraud is for professionals who take integrity seriously. A few ground rules:

  1. Each participant must register with their own name, company, and email.
  2. Scenarios should be completed individually — collaboration is great for learning, but individual scores reflect individual work.
  3. Do not share answer keys or internal scoring logic outside your team.
  4. Respect confidentiality: do not share any real client, company, or case data in scenario discussions.
  5. We reserve the right to adjust scoring models, categories, and recognition to keep the contest fair and meaningful.

Full terms and conditions will be available at registration and may vary by jurisdiction.
